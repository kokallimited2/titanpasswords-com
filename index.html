Protecting Your Most Critical Accounts: Email, Banking, and Identity

Not all accounts need the same level of protection. While a forum account with a weak password is low-risk, your email and banking accounts demand maximum security.

Why Your Email Is the Master Key

Your email account is the most important account you own. If someone gains access, they can reset passwords for every other account you have. This is why your email password should be your strongest, most unique password—at least 20 characters with full complexity.

Banking and Financial Security

Online banking accounts need extra protection beyond strong passwords. Always enable 2FA, use a unique password (never reuse your email password), consider using a dedicated device or browser for banking, and monitor accounts regularly for unauthorized activity.

The Three-Tier Password Strategy

Use different password standards: Tier 1 (Critical: email, banking, password manager) - 20+ characters, full complexity, unique. Tier 2 (Important: social media, shopping) - 16+ characters. Tier 3 (Standard: forums, newsletters) - 12+ characters.

12-16 characters for most accounts. 20+ for critical accounts. Every extra character helps!
